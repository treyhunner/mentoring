Coaching Resources

Resources on human connection, emotional intelligence, and coaching-related abilities

📺 “Emotional Intelligence for Engineers” by April Wensel

Tips on improving your workplace communication

📺 “Why Generosity Turns To Rage, And What To Do About It” by A. Jesse Jiryu Davis

Musings and advice on keeping your cool while attempting to be helpful

📺 “You're an expert. Here's how to teach like one” by Shannon V Turner

Helpful for coaches, conference speakers, tutorial presenters, and teachers in general

📺 “Those Who Care, Teach!” by Kenneth Love

Tips for folks teaching in a group setting

📺 “The power of vulnerability” by Brené Brown

The willingness to be vulnerable is powerful, both for you and for those around you

📑 “User's Manual” from the Recurse Center

A set of guidelines with helpful advice applicable to coaching and related settings

Teaching/Pedagogy Resources

Resources that have helped me improve my teaching and coaching practices

📖 “Make it Stick: The Science of Successful Learning” by Peter C. Brown, et al.

Practical advice drawn from cognitive science and learning/teaching theories

📖 “Peak: Secrets from the New Science of Expertise” by K. Anders Ericsson, Robert Pool

Includes tips on using "deliberate practice" for more effective learning

📖 “A Mind for Numbers” by Barbara Oakley

Targeted at learners: includes skills to encourage your learners to internalize

📺 “The power of believing that you can improve” by Carol Dweck

Carol Dweck started the initial research on the "growth mindset" phenomenon

Code Review Resources

Resources on how to perform helpful and effective code reviews

📺 “Code Review Skills for Pythonistas” by Nina Zakharenko

Primarily about submitting code, but the last 10 minutes focus on reviewing code

📺 “Code Reviews Using Art Critique Principles” by Stacy Morse

An analogy between art critiques and effective code reviews

📺 “Code Review, Forwards and Back” by Sumana Harihareswara, Jason Owen

Short theatrical plays of different code review scenarios to reflect on
